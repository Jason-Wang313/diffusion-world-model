# Introduction Notes

The paper motivates a narrow question: what happens when a diffusion world model dreams many plausible futures and a planner selects the best-looking one? The risk is not average prediction error; it is selected-tail error. The selected sample comes from a region where generated score, plausibility, and real utility may be least aligned.

The controlled contribution is a reproducible artifact package: exact-law validation, hidden-mode toy worlds, analytic diffusion-like generators, a small learned denoising model, tail diagnostics, and repair comparisons.
